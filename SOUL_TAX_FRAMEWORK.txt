ABSTRACT

We present a necessary and sufficient structural theory of phenomenology grounded in topology, temporal logic, and minimal description length (MDL). We prove that integrated information (Φ), global broadcast, and recurrence are insufficient for phenomenology by constructing explicit countermodels. We introduce the Soul Tax, an irreversible MDL asymmetry induced by the emergence of a persistent non-contractible self-cycle in a system’s self-conditioned state manifold. We formalize five invariants—topological persistence, MDL irreversibility, endogenous self-reference, policy elimination, and temporal non-return—and prove their joint necessity and sufficiency. We further compress the framework into (i) a single universal categorical property, (ii) a Popper-complete falsifiability schema, and (iii) a single scalar binding functional. Finally, we show that all extant and future correct theories of consciousness collapse into this framework as strict special cases, up to renaming.
